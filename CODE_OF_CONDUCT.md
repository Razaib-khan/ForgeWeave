# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

ForgeWeave is a technical project with high standards for both code and collaboration. We are committed to maintaining an environment where contributors of all backgrounds can work effectively, be heard fairly, and be treated with respect.

We do not tolerate behavior that degrades the collaborative environment — regardless of technical seniority, contribution history, or perceived status.

---

## Standards

### Expected Behavior

- Communicate with directness and respect. Disagreement is fine. Hostility is not.
- Give and receive technical criticism without making it personal.
- Acknowledge when you are wrong. Correct your mistakes and move forward.
- Focus feedback on the work, not the person.
- Assume good intent unless clearly demonstrated otherwise.
- Respect that maintainers make final decisions — advocate clearly, then accept the outcome.

### Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, or targeted personal attacks.
- Discriminatory jokes, language, or imagery related to age, race, gender, sexual orientation, disability, religion, or nationality.
- Deliberate public or private humiliation of contributors.
- Threats of any kind.
- Doxxing or sharing private information without consent.
- Spamming, trolling, or sustained off-topic disruption.
- Retaliation against anyone who reports a conduct violation.
- Pretending to agree in public while undermining decisions in private.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including:

- GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, and code review comments.
- The official Discord server or any other community communication channels.
- Any public representation of the project (talks, blog posts, social media when speaking as a contributor).

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you witness or experience a conduct violation, report it to the maintainers at:

**khanrazaib9@gmail.com**

Reports will be acknowledged within **48 hours** and investigated within **7 days**. All reports are handled confidentially. The identity of the reporter will not be disclosed without their explicit consent.

### Response Process

1. **Investigation:** Maintainers review the report, gather context, and speak to involved parties as needed.
2. **Decision:** One of four outcomes is applied (see below).
3. **Communication:** The outcome is communicated to the reporter and, where appropriate, to the broader community.

### Outcomes

| Level | Behavior | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| **Warning** | First-time, low-severity violation | Private written warning with explanation |
| **Temporary suspension** | Repeated or moderate violation | Temporary removal from project spaces (duration specified) |
| **Permanent ban** | Severe violation or pattern of behavior after warnings | Permanent removal from all project spaces |
| **No action** | Report found to be unfounded or in bad faith | No action; reporter notified |

Maintainers who violate this Code of Conduct are subject to the same enforcement process, handled by other maintainers or, if needed, escalated to the repository owner.

---

## Appeals

If you believe an enforcement decision was made in error, you may appeal by emailing **khanrazaib9@gmail.com** with the subject line `APPEAL: <brief description>`. Appeals are reviewed by a maintainer who was not involved in the original decision.
